Bottleneck Analysis

Primary

No conversion mechanism exists

6,490 accounts at the top, zero at the bottom. No LGF, no gated content, no conversion events. This is a capture problem, not a demand problem. The accounts are engaging — the system has no way to convert engagement into a lead.

Secondary

Targeting quality dilutes the signal

The broad launch means the high-engagement list currently mixes out-of-ICP names with the pharma and finserv accounts we want. Focusing targeting concentrates engagement density on ICP-fit accounts and sharpens the routing list.

Tertiary

WebID prospects skew non-ICP

Either website traffic itself is non-ICP (possible Google Ads targeting issue on the incumbent partner's account) or WebID captures organic/referral visitors that differ from the LinkedIn ad audience. Google consolidation answers this question.

What the Organic Data Teaches Consistent across all three months

Scale

Video is the engine

Video ran 3.86% ER (June) to 5.0% ER (April) — the best combination of engagement and reach in the mix, and the format Impactable produces. Every zero-engagement post in June was an in-house image post, not video. The signal is unambiguous: more video, less static image.

Cybersecurity engages, AI reaches

Cybersecurity content: 5.59% ER at 185 avg impressions. AI content: 2.69% ER at 278 avg impressions. Security goes deep, AI goes wide. Use AI content on reach days (top of funnel) and cybersecurity on engagement days. This split maps directly to the two executive voices.

Solid engagement, thin reach

June engagement sat in the upper-middle quartile (ER Q3) but reach sat in the bottom quartile (Q1) at 111 avg impressions. The content connects with the people who see it — not enough people see it. This is the organic case for the paid amplification of top posts (already working at 8.7x) and for the engagement-to-outreach flow in the Activation tab.

Bill's four-week climb

Bill's weekly ER rose every week of April: 2.18% → 4.81% → 6.08% → 6.41%. The driver was the experience-led reframe hook. That trajectory is the proof the hook pattern works and should be the default structure — not a one-time spike.

Organic and paid are one system. Organic is the free testing ground: the cybersecurity-over-AI pattern, the video-over-image pattern, and the experience-led hook were all proven organically before they informed paid creative. The paid channel then scales the winners (TL video ads at 1.48% CTR, 8.7x demand-gen). The missing third piece — harvesting the engagement that organic generates — is the Activation opportunity.

The signature finding: video improves under repetition, image fatigues. TOF TL Video CTR climbed from 1.22% (7d) to 1.47% (90d) as frequency built to 3.6x — because each promoted post is different content. TOF Image CTR dropped 32% (0.71% → 0.48%) at 5.7x frequency — the same static creatives repeated. The audience is telling us which format builds trust over time.
78.3% of the cold audience has never seen a TL video ad. At current spend (~$750/mo on TOF TL Video), the campaign reaches ~2,500 new unique members monthly. Reaching 50% penetration takes 5-6 more months at this pace. Three levers: (1) reallocate image budget into video, (2) clean the 35K audience to ~15-20K ICP-fit (instantly doubles penetration %), (3) add Google to capture the demand LinkedIn can't reach.
The retargeting engine is healthy and pool-constrained, not budget-constrained. MOF TL Video hits 96.8% of the warm pool within 30 days, the pool is growing (1,427 uniques at 90d vs ~1,200 baseline), and CTR improves with frequency (1.60% → 2.33% at 8.3x). New members enter faster than they age out. The constraint is pool size — which grows through more TOF reach and website traffic.
